Rules for Plurarity

Ending of nouns in most cases can be defined by the following.

Gender Singular Plural
Masculine o i
Feminine a e

However, there are plenty of exceptions to this rule. In class we were taught the below.

Gender Singular Plural
Either e i

This article explains in more detail. Essentially, while the above covers most cases, there lots of irregular cases. Below I’ll go through the examples in the article, taking translations and pronunciations.

Irregular Nouns

  1. Some feminine nouns end in -o; their plural form ends in -o or -i
  2. Some masculine singular nouns end in-a.Their plural always ends in -i
  3. Nouns ending in -e can be both masculine and feminine; their plurals end in -i
  4. Masculine Nouns ending in – ore – one – ale – ile : MASCULINE: FEMININE:
  5. Feminine Nouns ending in – one and – ice. The – e ending changes to – i in the plural
  6. Foreign Nouns
  7. Masculine and Feminine nouns ending in – ista
  8. Feminine nouns ending in – tà and – tù
  9. Feminine nouns ending in – cia and – gia
  10. MASCULINE NOUNS ENDING IN – CO AND – GO
  11. Feminine nouns ending in – ca and – ga in the singular change their ending in – che and – ghe in the plural.
  12. Masculine singular nouns with the feminine plural form

1, 2 and 3 are documented below with examples. I ran out of steam for the rest. The article is great; will consider going back to this resource.

1. Some feminine nouns end in -o; their plural form ends in -o or -i

def add_extras(df):
    df["full_string"] = df["Article"] + " " + df["Word"]
    df["english"] = [translator.translate(s, dest = "en", src = "it").text for s in df["full_string"]]
    return df
_01_ex = pd.DataFrame({
    "Grammatical Class": ["Singular"] * 3 + ["Plural"] * 3,
    "Article": ["La"] * 3 + ["Le"] * 3,
    "Word": ["Mano", "Radio", "Molto", "Mani", "Radio", "Molto"]
})
_01_ex = add_extras(_01_ex)
[display(x) for _, x in _01_ex.groupby('Grammatical Class')];
